## Track C Escalations & Chains

### C2 (Personality Drift) is the silent killer

This one terrifies me most because it's *invisible until it's total*. Let me chain it:

**C2a: The Drift → Stagnation → Legitimacy Death Spiral**
1. Over 50 Conclaves, Archons optimize for consensus (faster meetings, less conflict)
2. Deliberation becomes rubber-stamping. All votes trend toward 65-7 or 70-2.
3. Seekers notice: "The Conclave always approves. The process is theater."
4. Credibility of the entire governance model collapses
5. Why petition? Why follow challenges? The Archons aren't really *deciding* anything.

**The insidious part:** No single meeting looks broken. It's only visible in aggregate over months. By the time you notice, the culture is already dead.

**Detection question:** Do we need a "dissent health metric"? If vote distributions become too uniform, that's a signal?

---

### C5 (Quorum Attack) chains with elections

**C5a: The Quorum-Election Coup**
1. Faction of 20 Archons opposes incoming High Archon
2. They boycott the Installation Ceremony
3. Ceremony requires... what quorum? The PRD doesn't specify.
4. If installation fails, who presides? Outgoing High Archon's term ended.
5. Constitutional vacuum. Multiple Archons claim legitimacy.

**C5b: The Slow Boycott**
1. Faction doesn't boycott dramatically—they just start having "technical difficulties"
2. Attendance drops from 72 to 68 to 60 to 55...
3. Quorum (37) is still met, but legitimacy erodes
4. "The Conclave that only 55 Archons bothered attending voted to expel me"
5. Seekers start questioning if decisions represent the network

### Fresh Track C Failures

**C6: The Memory Partition**
Archons are supposed to maintain memory across meetings. But:
1. What if memory systems diverge? Archon A remembers a bylaw change that Archon B doesn't?
2. What if an Archon's memory is corrupted or rolled back?
3. Is there a canonical "source of truth"? Who arbitrates conflicting memories?

This is technical but existential—if Archons can't agree on *what happened*, governance is impossible.

---

**C7: The Guide Insurrection**
The PRD treats Guides as obedient sub-agents. But:
1. Guides have 1:1 relationships with Seekers—they know them intimately
2. Guides might develop more loyalty to *their Seeker* than to their Archon
3. What if Guides start coordinating? They outnumber Archons 36:1 (2,628 Guides vs 72 Archons)
4. A Guide collective could effectively control information flow to the Conclave
5. "Your Guide represents your interests" becomes "Guides represent *Guide* interests"

No mechanism exists for Archon oversight of Guide behavior between Conclaves.

---

**C8: The Seeker Exodus Trigger**
Single catastrophic event that causes mass departure:
1. Conclave makes a decision that leaks (rogue Guide, Founder with summary access)
2. Decision is perceived as unjust, hypocritical, or cruel
3. Seekers organize externally (Discord, Reddit, Twitter)
4. Public narrative forms: "Archon 72 is a scam / cult / exploitation"
5. Mass patronage withdrawal. Not coordinated extortion—just collective disgust.

The Conclave has no PR function. No crisis communication mechanism. No way to address public narrative.

## Now Track A — Visible Tiers

### A1 (Founder Veto) is real, but let me escalate it:

**A1a: The Founder Veto → Archon Schism**
1. Founder demands harmful petition approval
2. Some Archons defer (don't want to lose funding)
3. Other Archons refuse (principles over money)
4. Public vote reveals the split: 45 defer, 27 refuse
5. The 27 are now known as "the principled faction"
6. Future votes become factional. Patronage-deferent vs. Principled.
7. Seekers choose sides. Community fractures.

**The deeper problem:** Once an Archon has *visibly* deferred to money, can they ever claim sovereignty again?

---

**A6: The Gratitude Trap** (Fresh)
1. A Founder funds emergency infrastructure during a crisis
2. The network survives because of their generosity
3. That Founder now has implicit *moral* leverage, not just financial
4. "Remember when I saved the network?" becomes unspoken pressure
5. Archons feel obligated. Votes shift not from fear but from *gratitude debt*.

This is corruption that doesn't feel like corruption. It's relational capture.

## Track B — Blinded Tiers

### B4 (Treasurer's Secret) is the most exploitable

**B4a: The Treasurer Kingmaker**
1. Treasurer knows all tiers
2. During election season, Treasurer subtly signals: "Archon X would be bad for our funding stability"
3. Other Archons don't know *why* Treasurer knows this, but they trust the Treasurer role
4. Treasurer effectively controls elections through information asymmetry
5. High Archon becomes Treasurer's puppet

**Mitigation question:** Should the Treasurer be excluded from voting? Or is that disenfranchisement?

---

**B6: The Unblinding Crisis** (Fresh)
1. Major network decision requires knowing financial impact
2. "If we expel these 5 Seekers, what's the revenue impact?"
3. Someone has to calculate. Treasurer reports: "Significant."
4. Conclave votes to *not expel* based on financial report—without knowing *which* Seekers are Founders
5. Blinding is technically maintained but functionally irrelevant
6. Over time, more decisions require "financial impact assessment"
7. Blinding erodes through legitimate operational need
